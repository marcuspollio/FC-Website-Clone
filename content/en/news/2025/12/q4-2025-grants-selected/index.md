The FPA members have voted for Q4 2025 grants.

tetektoza will [revamp and enhance the selection system](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FPA-grant-proposals/issues/65) around specific bug reports listed in the grant application. He will be awarded a grant of 3,000 EUR.

Pierre-Louis Boyer aka PaddleStroke received two grants, for maintaining and improving the [Assembly](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FPA-grant-proposals/issues/73) and [Sketcher](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FPA-grant-proposals/issues/74) workbenches, 3,000€ for each grant. Work on Assembly will include both new features and bugfixes, work on Sketcher will be focused primarily on bugfixes.

Morgan Allen received a grant to [get his KiConnect PCB to the first public release](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FPA-grant-proposals/issues/72) and then enhance its functionality. The workbench he's been working on provides bidirectional interoperability between FreeCAD and KiCADs v9+ API. Morgan will be awarded a grant of $5200 for the project.

Additionally, Aleksandr Prokudin will [update Ondsel Lens documentation](https://github.com/FreeCAD/FPA-grant-proposals/issues/67) to cover recently added features and write documentation for the Lens addon. Aleksandr will be awarded a grant of 1,000 EUR from the separate [Ondsel Onward fund](https://blog.freecad.org/2025/02/14/the-ondsel-onwards-fund/).

We'll be introducing substantial changes to the 2026 grant program. Stay tuned for updates!
